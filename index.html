1. Veta o rozklade modulov. Existencia.

Veta: Každý konečne generovaný modul \(M\) nad OHI \(R\) je izomorfný priamemu súčtu cyklických modulov.

Dôkaz:
  1. Nech \(e_1, \dots, e_n\) sú generátory \(M\). Definujeme surjektívny homomorfizmus \(\phi: R^n \to M\). Z vety o izomorfizme vyplýva \(M \cong R^n / Ker \phi\).
  2. \(Ker \phi\) je podmodul voľného modulu nad OHI, preto je voľný hodnosti \(m \leq n\). Nech \(v_1, \dots, v_m\) je jeho báza. Vzťah medzi bázami je daný maticou \(A\).
  3. Podľa vety o Smithovom tvare existujú bázy \(\{u_i\}\) pre \(R^n\) a \(\{w_i\}\) pre \(Ker \phi\) také, že \(w_i = d_i u_i\), kde \(d_1 | d_2 | \dots | d_m\).
  4. Dosadením do izomorfizmu:
    \[M \cong (R u_1 \oplus \dots \oplus R u_n) / (R d_1 u_1 \oplus \dots \oplus R d_m u_m) \cong \bigoplus_{i=1}^m R/(d_i) \oplus R^{n-m}\]
Zdroj: PDF str. 13

Theorem: Every finitely generated module \(M\) over a PID \(R\) is isomorphic to a direct sum of cyclic modules.

Proof:
  1. Let \(e_1, \dots, e_n\) be generators of \(M\). We define a surjective homomorphism \(\phi: R^n \to M\). From the isomorphism theorem, it follows that \(M \cong R^n / Ker \phi\).
  2. \(Ker \phi\) is a submodule of a free module over a PID, therefore it is free of rank \(m \leq n\). Let \(v_1, \dots, v_m\) be its basis. The relation between the bases is given by matrix \(A\).
  3. According to the Smith Normal Form theorem, there exist bases \(\{u_i\}\) for \(R^n\) and \(\{w_i\}\) for \(Ker \phi\) such that \(w_i = d_i u_i\), where \(d_1 | d_2 | \dots | d_m\).
  4. Substituting into the isomorphism:
    \[M \cong (R u_1 \oplus \dots \oplus R u_n) / (R d_1 u_1 \oplus \dots \oplus R d_m u_m) \cong \bigoplus_{i=1}^m R/(d_i) \oplus R^{n-m}\]

2. Veta o rozklade modulov. Jednoznačnosť.

Dôkaz:

Jednoznačnosť rozkladu sa dokazuje pomocou determinantových deliteľov \(\eta_k\). \(\eta_k(A)\) je najväčší spoločný deliteľ všetkých minorov (subdeterminantov) rádu \(k\) matice vzťahov \(A\).

  • Elementárne operácie (riadkové a stĺpcové) nemenia ideál generovaný \(\eta_k\).
  • Pre maticu v Smithovom tvare platí \(\eta_k = d_1 d_2 \dots d_k\).
  • Invariantné delitele sú spätne určené ako \(d_k = \eta_k / \eta_{k-1}\).

Keďže \(\eta_k\) sú invariantné, aj postupnosť ideálov \((d_1) \supseteq (d_2) \supseteq \dots\) je určená jednoznačne.

Zdroj: PDF str. 14-15
Proof:

The uniqueness of the decomposition is proven using determinant divisors \(\eta_k\). \(\eta_k(A)\) is the greatest common divisor of all minors (subdeterminants) of order \(k\) of the relation matrix \(A\).

  • Elementary operations (row and column) do not change the ideal generated by \(\eta_k\).
  • For a matrix in Smith Normal Form, \(\eta_k = d_1 d_2 \dots d_k\) holds.
  • The invariant factors are recovered as \(d_k = \eta_k / \eta_{k-1}\).

Since \(\eta_k\) are invariant, the sequence of ideals \((d_1) \supseteq (d_2) \supseteq \dots\) is also uniquely determined.

Source: PDF pages 14-15

4. Smithov kanonický tvar matice pre E.D. Jednoznačnosť.

Dôkaz jednoznačnosti:

Nech \(A \sim diag(d_1, \dots, d_r, 0, \dots)\). Definujme \(\eta_k(A)\) ako NSD minorov rádu \(k\).

\[\eta_k(diag(d_1, \dots, d_r)) = d_1 d_2 \dots d_k\]

Pretože \(\eta_k\) sú invarianty ekvivalencie, invariantné delitele \(d_k\) vypočítame jednoznačne:

\[d_1 = \eta_1, \quad d_2 = \eta_2/\eta_1, \quad \dots, \quad d_r = \eta_r/\eta_{r-1}\]
Zdroj: PDF str. 10
Proof of Uniqueness:

Let \(A \sim diag(d_1, \dots, d_r, 0, \dots)\). Define \(\eta_k(A)\) as the GCD of minors of order \(k\).

\[\eta_k(diag(d_1, \dots, d_r)) = d_1 d_2 \dots d_k\]

Because \(\eta_k\) are invariants of equivalence, the invariant factors \(d_k\) are calculated uniquely:

\[d_1 = \eta_1, \quad d_2 = \eta_2/\eta_1, \quad \dots, \quad d_r = \eta_r/\eta_{r-1}\]
Source: PDF page 10

8. Veta o báze Ker φ pre φ: F[x]^n → (F[x], V, A)

Veta 6.5: Jadro \(\phi\) je generované stĺpcami matice \((xI - A)\).

Dôkaz:
  1. Vieme, že \(x \cdot v = A v\). Teda \(x e_i - A e_i = 0\) v module, čo znamená \((xI - A) \subseteq Ker \phi\).
  2. Nech \(P(x) \in Ker \phi\). Použijeme delenie polynomiálneho vektora maticou \((xI - A)\) so zvyškom:
    \[P(x) = (xI - A) \cdot Q(x) + R\]
    kde \(R\) je konštantný vektor (zvyšok stupňa 0).
  3. Aplikujeme \(\phi\): \(\phi(P(x)) = \phi((xI - A)Q(x)) + \phi(R)\).
  4. Keďže \(P(x) \in Ker \phi\) a \((xI - A) \in Ker \phi\), musí platiť \(\phi(R) = 0\).
  5. Pre konštantný vektor platí \(\phi(R) = R\). Teda \(R = 0\). To dokazuje, že \(P(x)\) je násobkom \((xI - A)\).
Zdroj: PDF str. 22

Theorem 6.5: The kernel of \(\phi\) is generated by the columns of the matrix \((xI - A)\).

Proof:
  1. We know that \(x \cdot v = A v\). Thus \(x e_i - A e_i = 0\) in the module, which means \((xI - A) \subseteq Ker \phi\).
  2. Let \(P(x) \in Ker \phi\). We use polynomial division of the vector by the matrix \((xI - A)\) with a remainder:
    \[P(x) = (xI - A) \cdot Q(x) + R\]
    where \(R\) is a constant vector (remainder of degree 0).
  3. Apply \(\phi\): \(\phi(P(x)) = \phi((xI - A)Q(x)) + \phi(R)\).
  4. Since \(P(x) \in Ker \phi\) and \((xI - A) \in Ker \phi\), it must hold that \(\phi(R) = 0\).
  5. For a constant vector, \(\phi(R) = R\). Thus \(R = 0\). This proves that \(P(x)\) is a multiple of \((xI - A)\).
Source: PDF page 22

10. Diagonalizovateľnosť, Cayley-Hamilton.

Veta 6.17 (Cayley-Hamilton): \(\chi_A(A) = 0\).

Dôkaz:

Charakteristický polynóm \(\chi_A(x) = \det(xI - A)\) je súčinom všetkých invariantných deliteľov:

\[\chi_A(x) = d_1(x) d_2(x) \dots d_r(x)\]

Minimálny polynóm \(m_A(x)\) je rovný poslednému invariantnému deliteľovi \(d_r(x)\). Keďže \(m_A(A) = 0\) (z definície minimálneho polynómu) a \(m_A(x)\) delí \(\chi_A(x)\), musí platiť \(\chi_A(A) = 0\).

Zdroj: PDF str. 25

Theorem 6.17 (Cayley-Hamilton): \(\chi_A(A) = 0\).

Proof:

The characteristic polynomial \(\chi_A(x) = \det(xI - A)\) is the product of all invariant factors:

\[\chi_A(x) = d_1(x) d_2(x) \dots d_r(x)\]

The minimal polynomial \(m_A(x)\) is equal to the last invariant factor \(d_r(x)\). Since \(m_A(A) = 0\) (by definition of the minimal polynomial) and \(m_A(x)\) divides \(\chi_A(x)\), it must hold that \(\chi_A(A) = 0\).
